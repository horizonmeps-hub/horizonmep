Residential Alteration

206 Littleton Avenue | Newark, NJ

The Challenges

  • Upgrading electrical systems to support modern residential loads while maintaining compatibility with existing utilities.
  • Full compliance with the 2021 International Building, Mechanical, and Fuel Gas Codes and the 2021 National Electrical Code (NEC).
  • Clash-free routing of feeders, raceways, and mechanical equipment within limited basement and residential floor space.

The Horizon MEP Solution

  • Engineered a 120/208V, 3-phase electrical distribution system with high-efficiency LED lighting, emergency battery packs, and occupancy controls meeting ASHRAE 90.1-2019.
  • Specified GFCI-protected and weather-resistant exterior receptacles and detailed fire-rated wall penetrations with approved sleeves and seals.
  • Designed dedicated circuits for electric water heaters and exhaust systems with proper overcurrent protection and disconnecting means.
  • Produced detailed symbolic layouts and circuit schedules to reduce field conflicts and construction delays.

The Result

The project transitioned smoothly from design to construction with a permit-ready electrical and mechanical package that enhanced safety, energy efficiency, and long-term reliability.
