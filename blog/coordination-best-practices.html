MEP Coordination Best Practices for Multi-Family Projects

Engineering Insights • Multi-Family Design

Multi-family developments place heavy demands on MEP coordination. Repetitive unit layouts, tight ceiling spaces, and life-safety requirements leave little margin for error.

1. Start Coordination Early

Early coordination between architecture, structure, and MEP disciplines prevents downstream conflicts that are costly to resolve during construction.

2. Maintain Consistent Unit Stacking

Repeating unit layouts improve system efficiency and reduce coordination complexity. Deviations should be carefully reviewed across all trades.

3. Prioritize Vertical Shafts

Plumbing and exhaust shafts are common conflict points. Early shaft planning ensures adequate space for all systems without last-minute redesign.

4. Coordinate Life-Safety Systems Thoroughly

Fire sprinklers, alarms, and egress requirements must remain coordinated across all floors to satisfy both building and fire departments.

Coordination Insight:
Most RFIs originate from coordination gaps that could have been resolved during design rather than in the field.
