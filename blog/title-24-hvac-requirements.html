Title-24 HVAC Compliance: Common Mistakes Architects Make

Engineering Insights • California Energy Compliance

California’s Title-24 Energy Code plays a critical role in HVAC system design, yet many permit delays stem from avoidable coordination issues rather than complex calculations.

1. Treating Title-24 as a Late-Stage Requirement

Energy compliance is often addressed after layouts are finalized. This can force equipment changes, duct resizing, and rework late in the permit phase.

2. Mismatch Between Loads and Equipment

Reviewers frequently flag inconsistencies between HVAC load calculations and selected equipment efficiencies. Title-24 models must align precisely with mechanical schedules.

3. Incomplete Documentation

Missing forms, unsigned compliance reports, or incorrect climate zone inputs are among the most common reasons Title-24 submissions are rejected.

4. Poor Coordination Between Architecture and MEP

Envelope assumptions directly impact energy modeling. When architectural details change without updating the energy model, permit comments are inevitable.

Best Practice:
Successful Title-24 compliance starts with early MEP involvement and ongoing coordination as the design evolves.
